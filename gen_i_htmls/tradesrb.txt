<p>In Pokemon Red and Blue, there are a few times when you'll be asked to trade Pokemon with an non-player character. Some of the Pokemon available prove advantageous against nearby Gyms or story points, and some are only available in these versions through such trades. 
</p>

	<table class="tab" align="center">
<tr>
<td class="fooleft" colspan="2"><font size="3">Route 2: Mr. Mime</a></font></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="fooinfo" valign="top"><p>
<b>Location</b>: Route 2</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
The first in game trade you'll probably encounter is this one outside Diglett's Cave on Route 2. A man will offer you his Mr. Mime for your Abra. Though Abra can be hard to catch, Mr. Mime doesn't appear in the wild at all, making this a good deal. Mr. Mime has decent Special and Speed but poor physical stats and HP.
</p>

<b>Location</b>: Route 5</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
The underground tunnel (north exit) has a trainer who will trade Nidoran for Nidoran. This trade is recommended in Red where male Nidoran are plentiful but female are rare. Blue version offers a common female Nidoran but rare male Nidoran, thus making it a useless deal, unless you want an outsider Pokemon that grows quicker.

<b>Location</b>: Route 18</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
Lickitung can only be obtained in this trade. Though Slowbro are uncommon, it is a decent trade off in terms of Pokedex completion. Despite this, Slowbro are much more useful in battle than Lickitung, who doesn't evolve in this game and has very mediocre stats. The owner is found in the gate upstairs on Route 18.
</p>

</tr>
<tr>
<td class="fooinfo" valign="top"><p>
<b>Location</b>: Cerulean City</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
The house next to the Pokemon Center is home to an elderly man who will trade his Jynx for your Poliwhirl. As with Mr. Mime, Jynx has great Speed and Special but unremarkable HP or physical stats. However, it gets the very rare Ice Punch move and can learn Ice Beam. This in game trade exclusive Pokemon is well worth the Poliwhirl.
</p>

<b>Location</b>: Cinnabar Island</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
The Cinnabar Island laboratory contains a couple of people who'd like to trade with you. These trades are mostly useless as you can catch all of the Pokemon offered in the wild at approximately the same difficulty. Ponyta for Seel is only helpful against Blaine if you can't be bothered to go to the Seafoam Islands to find one yourself.
