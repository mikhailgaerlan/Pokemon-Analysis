<p><font size="3"><b><u>Gift Pokémon</u></b></font></p>
<p>Like previous games, there are a number of Pokemon that can be received from various characters in the game.  For the most part the ways described on the page are the only way to get these Pokemon. There are, however, some exceptions.</p>

Like in all games, once you have chosen your starter, your rival will pick the Pokémon that is super effective against yours.</p>

<tr>
<td class="fooinfo" valign="top"><p>
<b>Location</b>: Route 111</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
Down in the deepest corners of Route 111 are two special fossils hidden past the Desert Ruins. On the desert floor you will find the fossils of two prehistoric Pokemon. Only one can be chosen - the other sinks into the sand, never to be found again. When handed over at Devon Corporation in Rustboro, a revived Pokemon will be later available. Lileep derives from the Root Fossil and Anorith from the Claw Fossil. Both are decent in their own right with unique typings not offered on any other Pokemon.</p>

<tr>
<td class="fooinfo" valign="top"><p>
<b>Location</b>: Weather Institute</p>

<p><b>Method</b>:<br />
After you rescue the scientists in the Weather Institute they will bestow on you the mysterious Pokemon Castform, whom Team Aqua or Magma were after. This Normal-type Pokemon can learn a variety of different elemental attacks and even changes ability in different weather - Fire-type in the sunshine, Water-type in heavy rain, and Ice-type in hail. Unfortunately it has no Rock-type form for sandstorm.</p>
